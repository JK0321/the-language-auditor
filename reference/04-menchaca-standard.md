# Menchaca Standard: Insurance Code Compliance

**Case:** USAA Texas Lloyds Co. v. Menchaca (Texas Supreme Court, 2018)  
**Citation:** No. 14-0721  
**Key Standards:** Unfair settlement practices under Texas Insurance Code § 21.21, including:
- Failure to conduct reasonable investigation
- Failure to attempt good faith settlement when liability is reasonably clear
- Failure to promptly explain coverage basis
- Failure to timely affirm or deny coverage
- Misrepresentation of policy terms

---

## What the Court Said

The Texas Insurance Code defines "unfair settlement practices" that apply separately from bad faith.

An insurer can be liable for Insurance Code violations even if not liable for breach of contract or common-law bad faith.

Key unfair practices include:
1. Refusing to pay without reasonable investigation
2. Failing to attempt settlement in good faith when liability is clear
3. Failing to promptly explain denial
4. Failing to timely affirm/deny coverage
5. Misrepresenting policy terms

---

## What This Means for Investigators

Your investigative report must demonstrate:

1. **Reasonable Investigation** — Even if claim is ultimately denied, investigation must have been reasonable
2. **Good Faith Settlement Efforts** — If liability becomes clear, settlement must be attempted
3. **Prompt Explanation** — Decision (coverage yes/no) must be timely and explained
4. **Clear Denial Language** — If denied, the reason must be clear and traceable
5. **Accurate Policy Information** — Never misrepresent what policy says

---

## The Menchaca Facts

After Hurricane Ike, Gail Menchaca reported wind damage to her home.

USAA sent an adjuster who found "minimal damage."

USAA determined the damage was below the deductible, so no payment was due.

Five months later, Menchaca requested a re-inspection.

A second adjuster confirmed minimal damage.

Menchaca sued claiming USAA:
- Failed to conduct reasonable investigation
- Refused to pay without good faith settlement attempt
- Failed to explain coverage decision
- Misrepresented policy terms

**The court's key finding:**
- Jury found "Yes" to: "Did USAA refuse to pay without reasonable investigation?"
- Jury found "No" to: "Did USAA fail to comply with policy terms?"

**The ruling:** Even though USAA's coverage determination was probably correct (damage below deductible = no payment), the investigation itself may have been unreasonable OR the explanation was insufficient.

---

## Menchaca Violations

### Violation 1: Investigation NOT Reasonable

❌ **PROBLEM:** Claim denied based on insufficient investigation.

Example: Single adjuster visit, no follow-up on contradictions, no opportunity for claimant input, no documentation of search method, no consideration of claimant's position.

✅ **CORRECT:** Thorough investigation documenting:
- Initial inspection method [describe]
- Claimant's position [describe]
- Contradictions found [list]
- Investigation methods to resolve contradictions [describe]
- Claimant opportunity to provide additional information [document]
- Conclusion based on full investigation [explain]

### Violation 2: Failure to Affirm/Deny Promptly

❌ **PROBLEM:** Claim in limbo; decision not made within reasonable time.

Example:
- Claim filed: Month 1
- Investigation ongoing: Months 1-3
- Decision made: Month 7
- No explanation for delay

✅ **CORRECT:**
- Claim filed: Month 1
- Investigation conducted: Weeks 1-4
- Decision made: Week 5
- Claimant notified: Week 6
- [If time extended, document specific reason]

### Violation 3: Failure to Provide Clear Explanation

❌ **PROBLEM:** Claim denied but reason unclear.

"Your claim has been denied due to coverage issues. Contact us for details."
- Generic language
- No specific policy section
- No reference to facts
- Claimant must call to understand

✅ **CORRECT:** 
"Your claim for Hurricane Ike wind damage has been reviewed under Policy [number], Section [X].

Coverage determination: NOT COVERED for the following reason:

Policy requirement: Damage exceeds $[amount] deductible per Section [X]
Your claim finding: Estimated damage is $[amount], below deductible
Conclusion: No coverage applies because damage does not exceed deductible

If you dispute this finding, you may request an appraisal per Section [Y] of your policy."

### Violation 4: Misrepresentation of Policy Terms

❌ **PROBLEM:** Investigator states what policy says, but misrepresents it.

"The policy covers only wind damage directly caused by the named storm."
- Not what policy actually says
- Adds language not in policy
- Limits coverage incorrectly

✅ **CORRECT:**
"Policy Section [X] states: '[QUOTE EXACT POLICY LANGUAGE]'

This means: [Your interpretation, clearly marked as interpretation, not fact]

Based on this policy language and your claim facts: [Coverage decision]"

## Three-Part Test (Menchaca)

For each claim, verify:

### Part 1: Was Investigation Reasonable?

Did investigator:
- [ ] Gather all available information?
- [ ] Document investigation method?
- [ ] Consider claimant's position?
- [ ] Investigate contradictions?
- [ ] Give claimant opportunity to respond?
- [ ] Reach conclusion based on full investigation?

**If "No" to any:** Investigation may not have been reasonable.

### Part 2: Is Explanation Clear?

Did determination:
- [ ] Identify specific policy section?
- [ ] Quote or cite policy language (not paraphrase)?
- [ ] Reference specific facts?
- [ ] Explain reasoning from facts to conclusion?
- [ ] Inform claimant of appeal options?

**If "No" to any:** Explanation may violate Menchaca.

### Part 3: Was Decision Timely?

- [ ] Investigation completed within reasonable time?
- [ ] Decision made promptly after investigation?
- [ ] Claimant notified within reasonable time?
- [ ] No unexplained delays?

**If "No" to any:** Timeliness may violate Menchaca.

---

## Red Flags (Menchaca Violations)

🚩 Single inspection only, no follow-up  
🚩 Claimant's position not documented/considered  
🚩 Coverage decision made without clear explanation  
🚩 Policy language misquoted or paraphrased inaccurately  
🚩 Months-long delay in making coverage decision  
🚩 Generic denial language ("coverage issues exist")  
🚩 No documentation of investigation method  
🚩 Claimant not given opportunity to respond to findings  
🚩 Claim denied with no reference to specific policy section

## Insurance Code § 21.21 Specific Violations

| Prohibited Practice | What It Means | Investigator Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Refusing to pay without reasonable investigation | Denying claims without thorough review | MENCHACA VIOLATION |
| Failing good faith settlement attempt when liability clear | Not trying to settle after coverage is clear | GILES VIOLATION |
| Failing to provide reasonable explanation | Denying without explaining why | MENCHACA VIOLATION |
| Failing to promptly affirm/deny | Delaying decision beyond reasonable time | MENCHACA VIOLATION |
| Misrepresenting policy terms | Stating what policy says incorrectly | MENCHACA VIOLATION |

## Menchaca vs Giles vs Stoker vs Arnold

| Standard | Key Question | Applies When |
|----------|---|---|
| **Arnold** | Is report well-documented? | Writing the report |
| **Stoker** | Is denial reason valid? | Denying claim |
| **Giles** | Is payment timely after clear liability? | Liability is reasonably clear |
| **Menchaca** | Is investigation reasonable + explanation clear + decision timely? | Handling entire claim |

**All four can apply to the same claim.**

---

## Key Takeaway

Menchaca requires:
- Reasonable investigation (not perfect, but thorough)
- Clear, timely explanation (whether covering or denying)
- Accurate policy language (never misrepresent terms)
- Prompt decision-making (no unexplained delays)

Menchaca doesn't eliminate coverage denials.

Menchaca eliminates UNREASONABLE investigations, UNCLEAR explanations, and UNTIMELY decisions.

It protects claimants from sloppy work and hidden reasoning.

For investigators: Document everything. Explain clearly. Decide promptly. Quote policy accurately.
