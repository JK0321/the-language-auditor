# Stoker Standard: Coverage Denial + Reasonable Basis

**Case:** República Insurance Co. v. Stoker (Texas Supreme Court, 1995)  
**Citation:** 903 S.W.2d 338  
**Key Standard:** An insurer may deny a claim if a valid reason for denial exists, even if the stated reason is incorrect.

---

## What the Court Said

An insurer is NOT in bad faith if it denies a claim for the wrong reason, as long as a valid reason for denial also exists.

However: The insurer must have a REASONABLE BASIS for the denial.

---

## What This Means for Investigators

Every claim denial must satisfy TWO requirements:

1. **A valid reason for denial must exist** (coverage gap, policy exclusion, or other contractual basis)
2. **The investigator must document the reasonable basis for that denial**

The Stoker case does NOT mean investigators can be careless about reasoning. It means:
- If multiple valid reasons exist for denial, investigator can rely on any one
- If investigator's stated reason is wrong, but another valid reason supports the denial, bad faith may not apply
- BUT the denial itself must be supported by documented facts

---

## The Stoker Facts

The Stokers were in a car accident after a truck dropped furniture on the highway.

They claimed under uninsured motorist coverage.

The insurer's adjuster concluded: "The Stokers were more than 50% at fault, so UM coverage doesn't apply."

**The problem:** The actual reason UM didn't apply was that there was NO CONTACT between the Stokers' vehicle and the truck that dropped the cargo. UM coverage requires contact with an unidentified motorist's vehicle.

**The court's ruling:** Even though the insurer stated the WRONG reason for denial (fault percentage), the denial was still valid because a VALID reason existed (no contact = no UM coverage). Bad faith did not apply.

## Common Violations (Stoker Standard)

### Violation 1: Stating Wrong Reason, No Valid Reason Exists

❌ **CRITICAL:** Investigator states reason A for denial, but:
- Reason A doesn't exist in the facts
- No other valid reason exists either
- Coverage actually applies

This is bad faith under Stoker.

✅ **Correct:** Only deny if:
- A valid contractual reason exists, AND
- That reason is documented

### Violation 2: No Documentation of Reasonable Basis

❌ **PROBLEM:** Investigator states denial reason but provides no supporting facts.

Example: "Claim denied because claimant was at fault."
- No evidence provided
- No investigation documented
- No facts supporting fault finding

✅ **CORRECT:** "Claim analysis: Claimant at-fault determination based on:
- Police report [citation] states claimant entered intersection against signal
- Witness statement [name, date] confirms red light violation
- Traffic laws [cite] state entering on red = fault
- Comparative negligence analysis shows claimant 100% at fault for this intersection violation"

### Violation 3: Multiple Possible Reasons, No Articulation

❌ **PROBLEM:** Several reasons might support denial, but investigator doesn't identify which one(s) apply.

"Various coverage issues exist with this claim."
- Vague
- Unverifiable
- Doesn't establish reasonable basis

✅ **CORRECT:** "Coverage analysis:
- Question 1: Does loss meet policy definition of [specific coverage]?
  Finding: No. Evidence: [specific facts]
- Question 2: Does exclusion [cite] apply?
  Finding: Yes. Evidence: [specific facts]
Conclusion: Valid reason for denial: Coverage exclusion applies per policy terms."

---

## How Stoker Affects Investigative Language

Stoker doesn't say "document carelessly, as long as SOME reason for denial exists."

Stoker says: "If a valid reason for denial exists, insurer may rely on it—but that reason must still be documented and reasonable."

**For investigators:**
- You don't need to prove you chose the BEST reason for denial
- You DO need to prove SOME valid reason exists and is documented
- Vague statements of reason = potential vulnerability
- Specific documentation of facts supporting reason = defensibility

---

## Red Flags (Stoker Violations)

🚩 "The claimant is likely at fault" (no evidence provided)  
🚩 "Coverage issues exist" (no specific analysis)  
🚩 "Various exclusions may apply" (no determination of which ones)  
🚩 Stated reason contradicted by documented facts  
🚩 No factual basis for coverage determination  

---

## Self-Test: Does Your Denial Meet Stoker Standard?

For each claim you deny, ask:

1. ✅ Have I identified at least ONE valid reason for denial?
   - [ ] Yes, specifically: [state the reason]
   - [ ] No

2. ✅ Have I documented facts supporting that reason?
   - [ ] Yes, evidence includes: [specific facts]
   - [ ] No

3. ✅ Is that documented reason traceable and verifiable?
   - [ ] Yes, reader can follow: fact → analysis → conclusion
   - [ ] No

4. ✅ Did I consider alternative interpretations of facts?
   - [ ] Yes, and rejected them because: [specific reasons]
   - [ ] No

If you answer "No" to any question, your denial may not meet the Stoker standard. Add documentation before finalizing the denial.

---

## Stoker vs Arnold

| Standard | Arnold | Stoker |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Requirement** | Report must have reasonable documented basis | Denial must have reasonable basis if valid reason exists |
| **Focus** | Quality of investigation/documentation | Validity of coverage determination |
| **If stated reason wrong** | Failure of documented basis | Bad faith only if NO valid reason exists |
| **Applies to** | Any investigative report | Claim denials specifically |

---

## Key Takeaway

Stoker protects investigators who deny claims for valid contractual reasons—even if they identify the wrong reason at first.

But Stoker REQUIRES that:
1. A valid reason actually exists
2. That reason is documented
3. The documentation is specific and traceable

"Coverage denied" with no explanation does NOT meet Stoker standard.

"Coverage denied because exclusion X applies; evidence: [facts]" DOES meet Stoker standard.
