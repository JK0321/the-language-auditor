# Arnold Standard: Reasonable Documented Basis

**Case:** Arnold v. National County Mutual Fire Insurance Co. (1987)

**Core Principle:** Insurers must base coverage determinations on a reasonable documented basis.

---

## What the Court Said

An insurance company's coverage determination must be supported by documented investigation of the facts.

The insured is entitled to coverage under the policy unless the insurer can establish facts that clearly exclude the loss.

---

## What This Means for Investigative Language

Every significant fact claimed in a coverage determination must be:
1. **Documented** — Not just suspected or inferred
2. **Specific** — Not vague or general
3. **Traceable** — The reader can see where it came from
4. **Supported** — Supported by evidence, not assumptions

---

## Common Violations of the Arnold Standard

### Violation 1: Unsupported Conclusions
❌ "The claimant was being deceptive."
- Where's the evidence of deception?
- What specific facts support this?
- This is conclusion without documentation.

✅ "Claimant stated [specific fact]. Documentation shows [different fact]. This discrepancy [matters because...]."

### Violation 2: Vague Language
❌ "There were suspicious circumstances surrounding the loss."
- What circumstances?
- Why are they suspicious?
- Vague language fails the "reasonable documented basis" standard.

✅ "The following circumstances were documented: [list specific facts]. These are notable because [reasoned explanation]."

### Violation 3: Missing Sources
❌ "The neighbors reported strange activity."
- Which neighbors? What exactly did they say? To whom? When?
- Without these details, it's not documented.

✅ "Neighbor [name] stated on [date]: '[exact quote about what was observed].'"

### Violation 4: Inference Without Facts
❌ "The delay in reporting indicates consciousness of guilt."
- Delay in reporting could mean many things
- Inferring guilt from delay lacks reasonable basis
- What specific facts distinguish this from other explanations?

✅ "Claim reported [date]. Loss occurred [date]. Delay: [time]. Claimant explanation: [documented response]. Other similar claims in this area show reporting patterns: [comparison]."

---

## How to Meet the Arnold Standard

**For every significant claim in your report:**

1. State the fact clearly
2. Document its source (observation, document, testimony, expert)
3. Explain why it matters
4. Address alternative explanations
5. Identify missing evidence gaps

**Format:**

"Fact: [The specific claim]

Source: [Where this came from]

Supporting details: [Specific information that documents this fact]

Why this matters: [Relevance to the investigation]

Alternatives: [Other possible explanations]

Missing evidence that would clarify: [What additional facts would help]"

---

## Why This Matters

If your investigation lacks reasonable documented basis:
- ❌ Coverage decisions are challengeable
- ❌ Claims can be reopened
- ❌ Litigation risk increases
- ❌ Your report loses credibility

If your investigation meets the Arnold standard:
- ✅ Decisions are defensible
- ✅ Claimants can understand your reasoning
- ✅ Litigation risk decreases
- ✅ Your professional judgment is clear

---

## Self-Test: Does Your Report Meet Arnold Standard?

For each significant finding in your report, ask:

- [ ] Is this fact documented, not inferred?
- [ ] Is the source identified?
- [ ] Is the language specific, not vague?
- [ ] Could a reader verify this independently?
- [ ] Have I considered alternative explanations?
- [ ] Have I identified evidence gaps?

If you answer "no" to any of these, your report may not meet the Arnold standard.
