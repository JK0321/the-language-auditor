# Texas Insurance Code: Guide for Investigators

**Current as of:** 2026  
**Scope:** Unfair Settlement Practices, Unfair Claims Settlement Practices, Deceptive Practices relevant to insurance investigations

---

## Part 1: Unfair Settlement Practices (§ 21.21)

### § 21.21, Section 4(10)(a) — Unfair Settlement Practices

**Current Statutory Language:**

> "Engaging in any of the following unfair settlement practices with respect to a claim by an insured or beneficiary:
>
> (i) Misrepresenting to an insured or beneficiary relevant facts or policy provisions relating to coverage at issue;
>
> (ii) Failing to attempt in good faith to effectuate a prompt, fair, and equitable settlement of a claim with respect to which the insurer's liability has become reasonably clear;
>
> (iii) Failing to promptly provide to an insured or beneficiary a reasonable explanation of the factual and legal basis in the policy for the denial of a claim or the offer of a compromise settlement;
>
> (iv) Refusing to pay a claim without conducting a reasonable investigation with respect to that claim."

**Source:** Texas Insurance Code § 21.21, Section 4(10)(a)

---

### What Each Provision Means for Investigators

#### (i) Misrepresenting Policy Terms

**Prohibition:** Do not misrepresent facts or policy provisions related to coverage.

**What This Means:**
- Never state what policy says if you're not quoting/citing it accurately
- Don't paraphrase policy language as if it's definitive
- Don't add conditions that aren't in the policy
- Don't omit coverage provisions that apply

**Examples of Violations:**

❌ **VIOLATION:** "The policy clearly excludes this type of damage."
- Is it clear? Have you read the exact exclusion language?
- Have you considered whether claimant's situation fits the exclusion?
- This is interpretation presented as fact

✅ **CORRECT:** "Policy Section [X] states: '[EXACT QUOTE].' This exclusion applies to your claim because [specific facts match the exclusion language]."

❌ **VIOLATION:** "This type of loss is not covered under standard homeowners policies."
- Maybe true generally, but does THIS policy cover it?
- Are you stating YOUR interpretation as policy fact?

✅ **CORRECT:** "Your policy does not provide coverage for [specific loss type]. The policy defines covered losses as [quote]. Your loss does not meet this definition because [specific facts]."

---

#### (ii) Failing to Attempt Good Faith Settlement

**Prohibition:** Once liability becomes "reasonably clear," insurer must attempt good faith settlement.

**What This Means:**
- Investigation must move toward settlement, not prolonged delay
- Once facts show claim is covered, don't request unnecessary documents
- Don't string claimant along with endless follow-up requests
- Don't make artificial objections once liability is clear

**Examples of Violations:**

❌ **VIOLATION — Unreasonable Delay:**
- Liability clear on Day 10
- Claim still unpaid on Day 60
- No explanation for delay
- Claimant calls multiple times for status

✅ **CORRECT:**
- Liability clear on Day 10
- Settlement offer made by Day 15
- If payment pending, claimant is updated on timeline
- No artificial delays

❌ **VIOLATION — Unnecessary Documentation After Liability Clear:**
- Medical records clearly support coverage
- Insurer requests "additional records verification"
- Claimant resubmits same records
- Process repeats

✅ **CORRECT:**
- Medical records reviewed and sufficient
- Settlement amount calculated
- Claimant notified of coverage and payment timeline
- Payment made without unnecessary requests

---

#### (iii) Failing to Provide Reasonable Explanation

**Prohibition:** Insurer must provide clear, timely explanation of coverage decision (whether yes or no).

**What This Means:**
- Explain the factual basis (what facts were found)
- Explain the legal basis (which policy section applies)
- Be specific, not generic
- Provide explanation promptly, not months later
- If denial, explain why coverage doesn't apply

**Examples of Violations:**

❌ **VIOLATION:**
"Your claim has been denied. Contact our office for information."
- No explanation
- Claimant must call to understand why
- Generic denial language

✅ **CORRECT:**
"Your claim for [type of loss] on [date] has been reviewed under Policy Number [X].

**Coverage Determination:** DENIED

**Factual Basis:** Our investigation found [specific facts].

**Legal Basis:** Policy Section [cite] provides coverage for [what is covered]. Your loss does not qualify because [specific reason].

**Policy Language:** Section [cite] states: '[exact quote]'

**Your Options:** 
- Request appraisal per Section [cite]
- Appeal to our claims manager: [contact info]
- Consult an attorney

If you have questions about this determination, please contact [specific person, phone, email]."

---

#### (iv) Refusing to Pay Without Reasonable Investigation

**Prohibition:** Every claim denial must be based on reasonable investigation, not refusal to investigate.

**What This Means:**
- Don't deny claims without adequate investigation
- Document what investigation was conducted
- Show claimant had opportunity to provide information
- Demonstrate investigation methods
- Document findings

**Examples of Violations:**

❌ **VIOLATION:**
- Claim filed
- Single phone call to claimant
- Claim denied same day
- No investigation documented
- Claimant never asked for supporting documents

✅ **CORRECT:**
"Investigation Conducted:
- [Date]: Claim received, initial review completed
- [Date]: Claimant interviewed [phone/in-person]; statement documented
- [Date]: Supporting documents requested: [list specific documents]
- [Date]: Documents received and reviewed
- [Date]: Site inspection conducted: [findings documented]
- [Date]: Coverage determination made based on investigation findings
- [Date]: Claimant notified of determination

Investigation Findings:
[Document what was found]

Coverage Determination:
[Based on findings, coverage is APPROVED/DENIED because...]"

## Part 2: Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act (§ 21.21-2)

### § 21.21-2, Section 2 — Unfair Claim Settlement Practices

**Current Statutory Language:**

> "It is an unfair claim settlement practice for any insurer to:
>
> (a) knowingly misrepresent to any claimant relevant facts or policy provisions relating to coverage at issue;
>
> (b) fail to promptly, diligently, and in good faith to investigate or settle a claim submitted by a claimant;
>
> (c) fail to acknowledge and act, with reasonable promptness, upon communications with respect to claims arising under insurance policies;
>
> (d) fail to adopt and implement reasonable standards for the prompt investigation of claims arising under insurance policies;
>
> (e) fail to maintain, for a reasonable period, adequate records concerning the handling of a claim arising under an insurance policy;
>
> (f) fail to provide information and assistance to claimants in connection with claims;
>
> (g) make claim settlement offers based on an incomplete investigation;
>
> (h) inform a claimant of specific portions of an insurance policy in isolated fashion so as to mislead the claimant as to the coverage provided;
>
> (i) fail to inform claimants of the denial of a claim, or of their right to appeal or arbitrate adverse claim settlements."

**Source:** Texas Insurance Code § 21.21-2(2)

---

### What Each Provision Means for Investigators

#### (a) Misrepresenting Policy Provisions

**Same as § 21.21(10)(a)(i) — see above**

#### (b) Fail to Investigate/Settle Promptly and Diligently

**Prohibition:** Investigation must be thorough AND timely.

**What This Means:**
- Don't procrastinate on investigations
- Don't delay investigation without good reason
- Don't conduct superficial investigations
- Don't string claimants along

**Red Flags:**
🚩 Investigation takes months without explanation  
🚩 Claimant requests are ignored  
🚩 No investigator assigned to case  
🚩 Investigation sits in queue  

**Compliance:**
✅ Investigation assigned promptly (within X days of claim)  
✅ Investigator contacts claimant within reasonable time  
✅ Investigation completed within reasonable period  
✅ Findings documented and delivered promptly  

---

#### (c) Fail to Acknowledge and Act on Communications

**Prohibition:** Insurer must respond to claimant communications promptly.

**What This Means:**
- Acknowledge receipt of documents/communications
- Respond to claimant inquiries promptly
- Don't ignore claimant calls or letters
- Don't leave claimant in the dark

**Red Flags:**
🚩 Claimant calls; no return call  
🚩 Documents submitted; no confirmation received  
🚩 Questions asked; no response  
🚩 No status updates given  

**Compliance:**
✅ Acknowledge receipt of documents within [X] days  
✅ Return claimant calls within [X] days  
✅ Respond to written inquiries within [X] days  
✅ Provide status updates at reasonable intervals  

---

#### (d) Fail to Adopt Standards for Prompt Investigation

**Prohibition:** Insurer must have and follow investigation standards.

**What This Means:**
- Have written investigation procedures
- Follow them consistently
- Document standards are being met
- Demonstrate investigation quality standards

**For Investigators:**
- Know your company's investigation standards
- Document that you're following them
- Show investigation is reasonable, not rushed or delayed
- Maintain consistent investigation quality

**Compliance:**
✅ Investigation follows documented procedures  
✅ Investigation steps are documented  
✅ Quality standards are met (thoroughness, timeliness, objectivity)  
✅ Investigation is reasonably complete before decision  

---

#### (e) Fail to Maintain Adequate Records

**Prohibition:** Insurer must keep records of claim handling.

**What This Means:**
- Document investigation steps
- Keep copies of communications
- Record claimant contacts
- Maintain investigation notes
- Keep documentation for required period

**For Investigators:**
- Document everything you do
- Keep investigation file complete
- Record dates and times of contacts
- Preserve all communications
- Maintain files for retention period

**Red Flag:**
🚩 No documentation of investigation  
🚩 No record of claimant contacts  
🚩 Investigation notes missing or destroyed  
🚩 No trail of decision-making  

**Compliance:**
✅ Every step documented  
✅ Dates and times recorded  
✅ Communications preserved  
✅ Decision reasoning documented  
✅ Files retained per policy  

---

#### (f) Fail to Provide Information and Assistance

**Prohibition:** Insurer must help claimants understand process and options.

**What This Means:**
- Explain claim process to claimant
- Provide information about coverage
- Help claimant understand requirements
- Don't make it difficult for claimant to comply

**For Investigators:**
- Explain what you need and why
- Tell claimant what to expect
- Provide contact information
- Make it easy for claimant to provide information
- Help claimant understand decisions

**Red Flag:**
🚩 Claimant doesn't know claim status  
🚩 Claimant doesn't understand what's needed  
🚩 No explanation of investigation timeline  
🚩 Claimant confused about coverage  

**Compliance:**
✅ Explain investigation steps to claimant  
✅ Tell claimant what documents are needed (specifically)  
✅ Provide timeline expectations  
✅ Explain coverage and why it applies/doesn't apply  
✅ Provide appeal process information  

---

#### (g) Make Settlement Offers Based on Incomplete Investigation

**Prohibition:** Investigation must be reasonably complete before offering settlement.

**What This Means:**
- Don't settle without adequate investigation
- Don't offer low settlement because you haven't investigated
- Complete investigation first, then settle based on findings
- Don't leave major questions unanswered

**For Investigators:**
- Complete investigation before sending findings to claims department
- If settling, ensure investigation is thorough enough to support settlement amount
- Don't cut investigation short just to close claim quickly

**Red Flag:**
🚩 Settlement offered before key facts determined  
🚩 Investigator not involved in settlement decision  
🚩 Investigation incomplete but claim settled  
🚩 Claimant paid without full investigation  

**Compliance:**
✅ Investigation completed before settlement  
✅ All key facts determined  
✅ Investigation documented  
✅ Settlement supported by investigation findings  

---

#### (h) Isolating Policy Provisions to Mislead

**Prohibition:** Don't quote policy sections out of context to mislead.

**What This Means:**
- Don't emphasize one exclusion while hiding coverage
- Don't quote definition fragments
- Don't isolate limiting language without full context
- Provide full policy picture

**Examples of Violations:**

❌ **VIOLATION:**
"The policy states losses must be 'sudden and accidental.' Your loss does not qualify."
- Quote taken out of context
- Other policy language might apply to this loss
- Misleading use of isolated language

✅ **CORRECT:**
"The policy provides coverage for 'sudden and accidental' losses as defined in Section [X]. Your loss is/is not covered under this definition because [explain why loss does/doesn't fit]."

---

#### (i) Fail to Inform of Denial/Appeal Rights

**Prohibition:** When claim is denied, must inform claimant of right to appeal.

**What This Means:**
- If denying claim, state that clearly
- Explain right to appeal or request reconsideration
- Provide appeal process and contact information
- Don't hide the fact that claim was denied

**For Investigators:**
- Your report should include clear coverage determination (APPROVED/DENIED)
- Explain appeal options available to claimant
- Provide contact info for appeals
- Make it clear claimant can challenge decision

**Red Flag:**
🚩 Denial not clearly stated  
🚩 No mention of appeal rights  
🚩 Appeal process not explained  
🚩 No contact info for appeals  

**Compliance:**
✅ Clear DENIAL statement  
✅ Explanation of appeal right  
✅ Appeal process steps explained  
✅ Contact information provided  
✅ Timeline for appeal given

## Part 3: How Texas Insurance Code Violations Show Up in Reports

### Common TIC Violations in Investigative Language

**Violation 1: Misrepresenting Policy (§ 21.21(10)(a)(i) + § 21.21-2(2)(a))**

❌ Report says: "Policy excludes damage from this type of loss."
- Is this actually quoted from policy?
- Or is it your interpretation?
- Could be misleading if isolation of exclusion hides coverage

✅ Better: "Policy Section [X] states: '[QUOTE].' This exclusion applies to your claim because [specific facts match exclusion]."

---

**Violation 2: Refusing to Investigate (§ 21.21(10)(a)(iv))**

❌ Report says: "Claim denied." (No investigation documented)
- No investigation steps shown
- No facts documented
- No reasoning shown

✅ Better: "Investigation Conducted:
- [Date]: Initial review
- [Date]: Claimant interviewed
- [Date]: Documents reviewed
- [Date]: Site inspection conducted
Finding: [Facts established]
Coverage determination: [Decision based on facts]"

---

**Violation 3: Incomplete Investigation Before Settlement (§ 21.21-2(2)(g))**

❌ Report says: "Recommend settlement of $X based on preliminary review."
- Investigation not complete
- Key facts still outstanding
- Settlement amount not supported by full investigation

✅ Better: "Investigation completed. All facts established:
- [Fact 1]: [Source]
- [Fact 2]: [Source]
- [Fact 3]: [Source]
Based on complete investigation: Recommend settlement of $X."

---

**Violation 4: Failing to Explain Denial (§ 21.21(10)(a)(iii))**

❌ Report says: "Coverage issues exist. Claim denied."
- No explanation of what coverage issues
- No policy section cited
- No reasoning shown
- Claimant won't understand why

✅ Better: "Coverage Analysis:
- Policy Section [X] requires [specific condition]
- Your loss: [facts of loss]
- Analysis: Loss does/does not meet policy requirement because [specific reason]
- Determination: DENIED because [specific coverage issue]"

---

**Violation 5: Misleading Isolation of Policy Language (§ 21.21-2(2)(h))**

❌ Report says: "Policy definition is narrow: 'sudden losses only.' Your loss does not qualify."
- Quote used out of context
- Other policy provisions not considered
- Misleading use of language

✅ Better: "Policy Section [X] provides coverage for:
1. 'Sudden losses' — defined as [full definition]
2. AND '[Other condition]' — defined as [full definition]
3. EXCEPT [exclusions listed]

Your loss: [describe facts]
Coverage analysis: Your loss is/is not covered because [explain how facts fit/don't fit policy language]"

---

## Part 6: Private Right of Action

### § 21.21, Section 16 — Private Right of Action

**Current Statutory Language:**

> "A person who sustains actual damages because of an act or practice in violation of this section may bring an action against the insurer to recover:
> 
> (1) actual damages;
> (2) court costs and reasonable attorney's fees;
> (3) interest on the actual damages at the rate allowed by law for judgment debts; and
> (4) exemplary damages, which may be recovered only if the insurer committed an unfair settlement practice **knowingly or with reckless disregard** for the law or the rights, safety, or health of others."

**Source:** Texas Insurance Code § 21.21, Section 16

---

## What This Means for Investigators

If your investigative report violates TIC:

1. **Claimant can sue for actual damages** (amount they were harmed)
2. **Claimant can recover attorney's fees** (insurers pay for claimant's lawyer)
3. **Claimant can get interest** (on top of damages)
4. **Claimant can get exemplary damages** IF violation was "knowing or reckless"

**Translation:** Sloppy investigation reports can be EXPENSIVE.

Good investigation = compliant with TIC = protects organization (and you).

---

## Bottom Line: Texas Insurance Code for Investigators

The TIC requires three things your investigative report must demonstrate:

1. **Thorough Investigation** — Before making any coverage determination
2. **Clear Explanation** — Of the decision and reasoning
3. **Accurate Policy Language** — Quoted or precisely cited, not paraphrased

Your report is the evidence the insurer relied on.

If your report violates TIC, the insurer's decision is vulnerable, regardless of whether the decision was correct.

Reports that comply with TIC:
- ✅ Support coverage determinations
- ✅ Protect the organization from liability
- ✅ Demonstrate professional judgment
- ✅ Are defensible in litigation

Reports that violate TIC:
- ❌ Expose organization to liability
- ❌ Create TIC violation claims
- ❌ Result in attorney's fees awards
- ❌ Damage professional reputation

**Choose compliance.**

## Quick Reference: TIC Compliance Checklist

Before you finalize ANY investigative report:

- [ ] Investigation thoroughly documented (§ 21.21-2(2)(b)(d)(e))
- [ ] Policy language quoted/cited accurately, not misrepresented (§ 21.21(10)(a)(i))
- [ ] Coverage decision clearly explained with factual + legal basis (§ 21.21(10)(a)(iii))
- [ ] Investigation completed before decision (§ 21.21(10)(a)(iv))
- [ ] Claimant communications responded to (§ 21.21-2(2)(c))
- [ ] Information provided to help claimant understand (§ 21.21-2(2)(f))
- [ ] Appeal/denial rights explained if applicable (§ 21.21-2(2)(i))
- [ ] No misleading isolation of policy language (§ 21.21-2(2)(h))
- [ ] Records maintained and complete (§ 21.21-2(2)(e))

**If all checked: Report is TIC-compliant.**
