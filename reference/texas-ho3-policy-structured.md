# Texas HO-3 Homeowners Insurance Policy
## Structured Version for Investigation Validation

**Form:** HO 00 03 10 00 - Homeowners 3 (Special Form)  
**State:** Texas  
**Purpose:** Reference policy for validating investigation report alignment to specific policy provisions

Use this index to find relevant policy language for investigation findings:

## SECTION I: PROPERTY COVERAGES

### Coverage A – Dwelling

**What is Covered:**
- The dwelling on the residence premises shown in Declarations, including structures attached to the dwelling
- Materials and supplies located on or next to the residence premises used to construct, alter or repair the dwelling or other structures

**What is NOT Covered:**
- Land, including land on which the dwelling is located

**Key Investigation Point:** Clarify whether damage is to the dwelling structure itself or to personal property inside.

---

### Coverage B – Other Structures

**What is Covered:**
- Other structures on the residence premises set apart from the dwelling by clear space
- Structures connected to the dwelling by only a fence, utility line, or similar connection

**What is NOT Covered:**
- Land
- Other structures rented or held for rental to any person not a tenant of the dwelling (unless used solely as private garage)
- Other structures from which any "business" is conducted
- Other structures used to store "business" property

**Limit:** Not more than 10% of the limit of liability that applies to Coverage A

**Key Investigation Point:** Verify whether structure is truly "other structure" or part of dwelling; confirm no business use.

---

### Coverage C – Personal Property

**What is Covered:**
- Personal property owned or used by an "insured" while it is anywhere in the world
- After a loss and at your request, property owned by others while on insured's premises or in any residence occupied by insured (guests, residence employees)

**Special Limits of Liability (Total per loss, per category):**
- $200 on money, bank notes, bullion, coins, medals, scrip, stored value cards
- $1,500 on securities, accounts, deeds, evidence of debt, letters of credit, notes, manuscripts, personal records, passports, tickets, stamps
- $1,500 on watercraft of all types, including trailers, furnishings, equipment and outboard engines or motors
- $1,500 on trailers or semitrailers not used with watercraft
- **$1,500 for loss by THEFT of jewelry, watches, furs, precious and semiprecious stones**
- **$2,500 for loss by THEFT of firearms and related equipment**
- **$2,500 for loss by THEFT of silverware, silver-plated ware, goldware, gold-plated ware, platinumware, platinum-plated ware and pewterware**
- $2,500 on property on the residence premises used primarily for "business" purposes
- $500 on property away from the residence premises used primarily for "business" purposes
- $1,500 on electronic apparatus and accessories while in or upon a "motor vehicle"
- $1,500 on electronic apparatus and accessories used primarily for "business" while away from residence premises

**Limit for Property at Other Residences:**
- 10% of Coverage C limit, or $1,000, whichever is greater (with exceptions for property being repaired/renovated and newly acquired residences)

**What is NOT Covered:**
- Articles separately described and specifically insured in other insurance
- Animals, birds or fish
- Motor vehicles and their accessories, equipment, and parts
- Articles in or upon vehicles designed for use off public roads or on farms
- Excluded electronic apparatus and accessories designed to be operated solely by power from motor vehicle

**Key Investigation Points:**
- Verify exact items claimed and their location at time of loss
- Check if items claimed exceed special limits for category
- Confirm items are not separately insured elsewhere
- Verify items are not "business property"

---

### Special Provisions for Loss by Theft

**Special Limits Apply to:**
- Jewelry, watches, furs, precious and semiprecious stones: **$1,500 per loss**
- Firearms and related equipment: **$2,500 per loss**
- Silverware and similar items: **$2,500 per loss**

**Investigation Note:** Claims involving theft of these categories require verification that claim does not exceed special limit.

## SECTION I: PERILS INSURED AGAINST

**Covered Perils (property is covered against direct physical loss to property, including the following perils):**

1. **Fire or Lightning**
2. **Windstorm or Hail** (subject to deductible)
3. **Explosion**
4. **Riot or Civil Commotion**
5. **Aircraft** (including self-propelled missiles and spacecraft)
6. **Vehicles** (not owned by occupants of the residence premises)
7. **Smoke**
8. **Vandalism**
9. **Sprinkler Leakage** (if system is installed to protect the property)
10. **Sudden and Accidental Tearing Apart, Cracking, Burning or Bulging** (of steam, hot water, air conditioning or automatic fire protective sprinkler system, or of appliances for heating water)
11. **Freezing** of pipes, fixtures and plumbing systems, air conditioning systems
12. **Sudden and Accidental Water Damage** from plumbing, heating, air conditioning systems, or domestic appliances
13. **Falling Objects**
14. **Weight of Ice, Snow or Sleet**
15. **Accidental Discharge or Overflow of Water**
16. **Sudden and Accidental Straining, Cracking, Ripping or Tearing** (of water heaters, boilers, or air conditioning systems)
17. **Freezing, Thawing, Pressure or Rupture** (of plumbing, heating, air conditioning systems)
18. **Artificially Generated Electricity**
19. **Volcanic Action**

## SECTION I: EXCLUSIONS

**Water-Related Exclusions (Critical for Water Damage Claims):**

The following water damage is NOT covered:
1. Water below the surface of the ground, including water that exerts pressure on, or seeps or leaks through building, pavement, or other surface
2. Water which backs up into or overflows from a sump, sump pump, or related equipment
3. Fungus, wet rot, dry rot, bacteria
4. Restoration of property that has been damaged by fungus, wet rot, dry rot, or bacteria

**BUT — The following water damage IS covered (Important Exception):**
- Water damage from plumbing systems, heating systems, air conditioning systems, or domestic appliances, IF the damage does not result from freezing, thawing, pressure or rupture (which have a separate peril)
- Sudden and accidental water damage from these systems

**Theft Exclusions:**
- Theft committed by insured or any other resident of the residence premises
- Theft from the residence premises for an insured under age 21 unless they are your relative and reside full-time at the residence premises

**Fire/Explosion Exclusions:**
- Loss to property, including debris removal, caused by fire if the property is destroyed or damaged by fire made more severe by any condition of the property existing before the fire

**Business Exclusions:**
- Property used for business purposes
- Business property and inventory

**Other Key Exclusions:**
- Loss caused by wear and tear, marring, deterioration
- Loss to property left unattended in a vehicle
- Loss to property in an unoccupied building for more than 30 consecutive days
- Loss to property struck by equipment used to construct, renovate, repair or demolish property
- Loss by theft of property in or upon a vehicle (with exceptions)

**Investigation Note:** Exclusions are critical to denial decisions. Investigation must establish whether loss falls under an excluded cause or has an excluded characteristic.

## SECTION I: CONDITIONS

### A. Insurable Interest and Limit of Liability

- We will not be liable in any one loss to an "insured" for more than the amount of such insured's interest at the time of loss
- We will not be liable for more than the applicable limit of liability

---

### B. Duties After Loss — CRITICAL SECTION FOR INVESTIGATION

**These duties must be performed by you, an "insured" seeking coverage, or a representative of either:**

**1. Prompt Notice**
- Give prompt notice to us or our agent of loss
- Investigation Note: Insurer must show that delayed notice was "prejudicial" (harmful) to deny claim

**2. Police Notification**
- Notify the police in case of loss by theft

**3. Protect Property from Further Damage**
- Make reasonable and necessary repairs to protect property
- Keep an accurate record of repair expenses

**4. Cooperation with Investigation**
- **Cooperate with us in the investigation of a claim**
- Investigation Note: This is the basis for denial if insured refuses to cooperate

**5. Inventory of Damaged Property**
Prepare an inventory of damaged personal property showing:
- Quantity
- Description
- Actual cash value
- Amount of loss
- Attach all bills, receipts and related documents justifying the figures

**6. Provide Records and Documents — On Demand**
As often as we reasonably require:
- Show the damaged property
- Provide us with records and documents we request and permit us to make copies
- Submit to examination under oath, while not in the presence of another "insured"

**7. Proof of Loss — MANDATORY WITHIN 60 DAYS**

**Send to us, within 60 days after our request, your signed, sworn proof of loss which sets forth, to the best of your knowledge and belief:**
- a. The time and cause of loss
- b. The interests of all "insureds" and all others in the property involved and all liens on the property
- c. Other insurance which may cover the loss
- d. Changes in title or occupancy of the property during the term of the policy
- e. Specifications of damaged buildings and detailed repair estimates
- f. The inventory of damaged personal property (from #5 above)
- g. Receipts for additional living expenses incurred and records that support the fair rental value loss
- h. Evidence or affidavit that supports a claim

**Investigation Note:** Proof of loss must be signed and sworn. This is a material requirement. Non-compliance or false statements can void coverage.

---

### C. Loss Settlement

**Actual Cash Value Settlement:**
- Personal property
- Awnings, carpeting, household appliances, outdoor antennas and equipment
- Structures that are not buildings
- **Settled at actual cash value at the time of loss, but not more than the amount required to repair or replace**

**Replacement Cost Settlement (for dwelling):**
- Buildings covered under Coverage A or B at replacement cost without deduction for depreciation
- **IF insurance is 80% or more of full replacement cost:** We pay the cost to repair or replace, after deductible and without depreciation, but not more than:
  - The limit of liability under this policy that applies to the building
  - The replacement cost of that part of the building damaged
  - The necessary amount actually spent to repair or replace
- **IF insurance is less than 80% of full replacement cost:** We apply a penalty (pro-rata recovery)

**Investigation Note:** Confirm replacement cost valuations meet 80% threshold. Valuations below this trigger penalty clause.

## SECTION I: DEFINITIONS (Key Terms for Investigation)

**"Insured"** means:
- You and residents of your household who are your relatives or other persons under 21 in your care
- Students enrolled full-time in school who were residents before moving (age limits apply)
- Any person or organization legally responsible for animals or watercraft owned by an insured

**"Insured Location"** means:
- The residence premises
- Other residences used by you as a residence
- Any premises used in connection with a residence premises
- Temporarily rented premises where you are temporarily residing
- Vacant land owned or rented by you
- Land being built upon for a residence
- Cemetery plots or burial vaults

**"Occurrence"** means an accident, including continuous or repeated exposure to substantially the same general harmful conditions, which results, during the policy period, in bodily injury or property damage

**"Residence Premises"** means:
- The one family dwelling where you reside, OR
- The two, three or four family dwelling where you reside in one of the units, OR
- That part of any other building where you reside
- **Also includes other structures and grounds at that location**

**"Residence Employee"** means:
- An employee related to maintenance or use of the residence premises, including household or domestic services
- Does NOT include temporary employees substituting for permanent employees

## INVESTIGATION VALIDATION CHECKLIST

Use this checklist when validating an investigation report against this HO-3 policy:

### Coverage Verification
- [ ] Loss falls within one of the named Coverages (A, B, or C)?
- [ ] If Coverage C (personal property), verify items are specifically covered
- [ ] Check if loss involves special limit category (jewelry $1,500, firearms $2,500, etc.)
- [ ] Confirm loss is not excluded (business property, water exclusions, theft exclusions, etc.)

### Peril/Cause of Loss
- [ ] Investigator identified the specific peril that caused the loss
- [ ] Investigator verified peril is listed as covered peril
- [ ] If water damage, investigator confirmed it's not excluded water (e.g., below-ground water, backed-up water, fungus)
- [ ] If theft, investigator confirmed it's not by insured or underage resident

### Insured's Duties
- [ ] Report examined whether insured gave prompt notice (policy doesn't define "prompt")
- [ ] Report examined whether insured cooperated with investigation
- [ ] Report examined whether insured protected property from further damage
- [ ] Report examined whether insured provided detailed inventory of damaged property
- [ ] Report examined whether insured provided bills, receipts, documentation
- [ ] Report examined whether insured filed sworn proof of loss within 60 days

### Valuation & Limits
- [ ] Investigator verified actual cash value (if settling as ACV)
- [ ] Investigator verified replacement cost meets 80% threshold (if settling as replacement cost)
- [ ] Investigator confirmed claim does not exceed applicable limit
- [ ] Investigator confirmed claim does not exceed special limit (if applicable)

### Policy Disclosure
- [ ] Report explicitly states which Coverage(s) apply (Coverage A, B, or C)
- [ ] Report explains how specific policy provisions affect the finding
- [ ] Report identifies if loss falls within or outside coverage
- [ ] Report identifies which policy exclusions apply (if any)

### Example: Water Damage Claim

**Good Report Language:**
"The insured reported water damage to the dwelling from a burst hot water heater. Coverage A (Dwelling) provides coverage for sudden and accidental water damage from domestic appliances. The policy excludes water damage below the surface of the ground, backed-up water, and fungus-related damage. Investigation confirmed: (1) water source is the hot water heater (covered peril); (2) damage occurred suddenly without fungus involvement; (3) water did not back up from plumbing (excluded scenario). Claim is **within coverage**."

**Poor Report Language:**
"The dwelling suffered water damage. Investigation shows the insured is claiming coverage."

## INTEGRATION WITH INVESTIGATION VALIDATOR

This policy should be referenced when reviewing investigation reports for:

1. **Finding ≠ Policy Impact** — Does the investigator explain which policy provisions the findings implicate?
2. **Alternative Hypothesis** — Did the investigator consider whether loss might be excluded?
3. **Precision Validation** — Are specific coverages and limits cited?
4. **Authority Boundaries** — Does investigator stay out of claims adjudication (that's insurer's job)?
5. **Evidence Traceability** — Can you trace from finding back to specific policy language?
